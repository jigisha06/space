After independence, India needed technology that would help in its growth. Space was the key to solving real-world problems: reaching remote areas with education, managing natural disasters, weather prediction, and agricultural monitoring.

ISRO was formed to build this capability in-house, without depending on foreign agencies. It aimed to:

  • Empower rural and urban development using space tech
  • Create independent launch systems
  • Use space for peaceful, developmental purposes
